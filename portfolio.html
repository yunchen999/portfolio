2. Does Upload Frequency Drive YouTube Creator Success?
Marketing Analytics | Statistical Hypothesis Testing | UIUC MSBA · 2026
Marketing Analytics Statistical Testing Data Visualization

Situation

A widely held assumption in the YouTube creator community is that posting more videos leads to faster channel growth. This project systematically tests that hypothesis using real data from 75 top creators across three content categories.

Task

Determine whether upload frequency (video count) is a statistically significant predictor of creator success (subscriber count), and investigate whether content efficiency varies meaningfully across categories.

Action

  • Collected channel data via YouTube Data API v3 for 75 top creators (25 per category: Entertainment, Education, Science & Technology).
  • Engineered derived metrics: subscribers per video (content efficiency) and views per video.
  • Built an OLS regression model to test whether video count predicts subscriber count.
  • Constructed a correlation matrix to examine pairwise relationships across all numeric variables.
  • Applied the Kruskal-Wallis test to evaluate whether subscriber efficiency differs significantly across categories.
  • Developed three Tableau dashboards: category overview, top-creator performance, and scatter/distribution visualizations.

Statistical Results

AnalysisKey MetricResult
OLS Regression (R²)Variance explained2.5% — extremely weak
OLS p-valueStatistical significance0.175 — fail to reject H₀
Correlation: subs ↔ videosLinear relationship−0.037 (near zero)
Correlation: subs ↔ viewsLinear relationship+0.93 (very strong)
Kruskal-Wallis p-valueCategory efficiency gap5.72 × 10⁻⁷ (highly significant)

Key Findings

  • 📉 Upload frequency explains only 2.5% of subscriber growth — the null hypothesis is not rejected.
  • 🎬 Content engagement (view count) is the dominant growth driver, with r = 0.93 vs. subscribers.
  • 🏆 Entertainment creators generate 52,416 subscribers per video — 12.6× more efficient than Science & Technology (4,150 subs/video).
  • ⭐ Quality outliers like Mark Rober (78M subs, 258 videos, 303K subs/video) confirm that strategic, high-quality content outperforms volume-driven approaches.
